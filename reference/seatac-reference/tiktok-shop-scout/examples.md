# Examples — Decisions in Action

Three real decision walkthroughs, one per output type. The third is an edge case the obvious rules don't directly cover.

---

## Example 1 — PROMOTE: Heatless curling ribbon kit

**Input:** Listing URL. $16.99, 14% commission stated on the affiliate marketplace, ~420 active creators, sales count up roughly 40% week-over-week, overnight before/after is the dominant video format.

**§1 disqualifiers:** none — beauty tool, fully demonstrable.

**§2 scoring:** Commission 4 (14% confirmed) · Velocity 4 (40% WoW signal) · Saturation 4 (~420) · Angle 4 — the generic "overnight curls" demo is everywhere, but the top videos all show it on long straight hair; "this is what it does on short/fine hair" is an underused variant with built-in comment bait · ICP fit 5 (beauty category #1; "visible before/after" trigger) · Price 5 ($17 impulse zone) → **26/30.**

**§3:** ≥18, commission confirmed → **PROMOTE.**

**Output (abridged):** Hook: "POV: fine hair that's never held a curl past lunch." Format: overnight before/after. Caption: `Fine hair girls, this one's actually for us. Tap the tag. #ad #TikTokShop #heatlesscurls #finehair`. Compliance boxes all checked.

## Example 2 — SKIP: Viral magnesium sleep gummies

**Input:** Screenshot description. Gummies, $22, 20% commission, "everyone on my FYP is posting these," sales clearly exploding.

**§1 disqualifiers:** DQ#1 — ingestible supplement. The benefit claim ("fall asleep faster") is a health outcome that can't be shown on camera and can't be captioned without prohibited language.

**Decision: SKIP.** Scoring never happens — 20% commission and exploding velocity are irrelevant once a disqualifier hits. This is the rule doing its job: the most tempting products are the ones the disqualifier list exists for.

**Rejection log entry:**
`| 2026-06-10 | Magnesium sleep gummies | DQ | DQ#1 ingestible — health-outcome claim required to sell it |`

## Example 3 — Edge case → WATCHLIST: Red-light facial wand timed to a viral skincare documentary

**Input:** $54 red-light cosmetic wand. Commission "up to 15%" (range stated, not confirmed). ~280 creators. Velocity spiking — but the spike traces to a skincare documentary that dropped on a streaming platform two weeks ago.

**Why the obvious rules don't cover it:** Two rules collide and one is ambiguous.
1. Is a documentary-driven spike DQ#4 (one-time news event → instant skip) or §4.3 (seasonal wave → score it with a post-by date)? The rules don't say.
2. It's wellness-adjacent (§4.4) *and* the commission is unconfirmed (§3).

**How the operator reasons it:** DQ#4 exists because news spikes die in days and aren't repeatable. A documentary behaves differently — it keeps acquiring viewers for weeks and the underlying product category (red-light skincare) existed before it and will exist after. So this is a *wave with a long tail*, closer to §4.3 seasonal than DQ#4. Not a skip.

**§2 scoring:** Commission 2 ("up to 15%" treated as estimate, scored one lower per rubric discipline) · Velocity 5 · Saturation 5 (~280) · Angle 4 ("I bought the $54 version so you don't need the $300 one" — price-anchor trigger) · ICP fit 5 · Price 4 → **25/30.**

**§3:** ≥18 but commission **estimated** → **WATCHLIST**, not promote. §4.4 also applies: when this flips, claims must stay cosmetic ("my skin looked brighter after two weeks"), never medical ("treats acne," "boosts collagen production").

**Watchlist entry:**
- **Flip condition:** commission confirmed ≥ 8% on the affiliate marketplace
- **Re-check:** 2026-06-17 (7 days — documentary tail won't wait 14)
- **If flipped:** promote with the price-anchor hook above; allowed claims constrained to cosmetic observations per §4.4
- **If not flipped:** rejection log, "watchlist expired — commission never confirmed"

The point: a high score, a hot wave, and a near-miss disqualifier — and the operator still produced one unambiguous decision with a measurable flip condition, instead of asking the human what to do.
