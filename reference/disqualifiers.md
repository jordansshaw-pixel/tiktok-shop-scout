# Hard Disqualifiers & Claim Rules

These are compliance rules, not preferences. No rubric score overrides them. If a product hits a disqualifier, it is SKIPPED before scoring.

## Instant disqualifiers

| # | Category | Why |
|---|----------|-----|
| 1 | Ingestible supplements (pills, powders, gummies, teas with health claims) | Claim risk — any benefit statement edges toward an unprovable medical claim |
| 2 | Products marketed on medical outcomes ("cures," "treats," "reverses," "lose X lbs in X days") | FTC prohibited claims |
| 3 | Electronics with unverifiable spec claims (battery capacity, "military grade," output wattage you can't test on camera) | Demonstrating fake specs = creator liability |
| 4 | Products trending off a one-time news event | Not repeatable; wave dies before content compounds |
| 5 | Products with no possible visual demo under 30 seconds | No demo = no video = no conversion |
| 6 | "Make money with this" products | Earnings claims are prohibited; implying viewer income is an FTC violation |

## The wellness-adjacent boundary (rules.md §4.4)

**Allowed** (benefit is physically demonstrable, no medical claim needed): massage guns, posture correctors, sleep masks, ice rollers, red-light cosmetic wands, acupressure mats.

**Banned** (benefit requires ingestion or a medical outcome): any supplement, detox product, weight-loss aid, hormone/sleep gummy.

The test in one line: *can the camera show the benefit without the caption making a health promise?*

## Claim language for promoted products

Every promote brief inherits these caption constraints:

- ✅ "Helps with," "I noticed," "my skin looked smoother after a week," "felt looser"
- ❌ "Clinically proven" (unless the specific study is cited), "doctor recommended" (unless documented), permanent-result claims, outcome promises with timelines

## Disclosure requirements (every promoted product, no exceptions)

- Caption must include `#ad` (plus `#TikTokShop` by convention) — visible before the "more" fold, not buried after 5+ hashtags
- Product linked via TikTok's native product tag — never a third-party affiliate link in the caption
- If the script is verbal-only, on-screen text overlay at video start: "Affiliate link | #ad"
